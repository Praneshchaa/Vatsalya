# Vatsalya Sewa Griha — Project Context for Website Design

## What is Vatsalya Sewa Griha?

Vatsalya Sewa Griha is an elder care home located in Kumaripati Ward-5, Lalitpur, Nepal. It provides a safe, comfortable, and socially active living environment for elderly people who need care, company, and daily support.

The name "Vatsalya" means parental love and affection in Nepali — which reflects the core spirit of the organization.

## What do they actually offer?

- A home-like residential environment for elderly people

- Health monitoring with trained medical staff on site
- Safe, clean, and comfortable living spaces

---

## The real positioning

Vatsalya is **not a hospital or nursing facility.** It is a community home where elders are active, social, and happy. The primary need families come with is not medical — it is that their parents are **lonely and inactive at home** while the family is at work.

Vatsalya solves loneliness, not just illness.

---

## Who is the website for?

**Primary audience:** Adult children (ages 30–55) living in Kathmandu valley who are working full time and are looking for a safe, engaging place for their elderly parent. They feel guilty about this decision and need reassurance that choosing Vatsalya is an act of love, not abandonment.

**Secondary audience:** Potential donors, volunteers, and partner organizations.

---

## What should the website do?

1. Build trust immediately — show real people, real spaces, real staff
2. Reframe the guilt — position placing a parent here as the loving, responsible choice
3. Make it easy to enquire — phone number and contact form always visible
4. Show the community feel — activities, meals, daily life, not just services
5. Introduce the staff — qualified, caring, experienced people families can trust

---

## Pages the website needs

- Home

---

## What the website should feel like

Warm. Calm. Trustworthy. Like visiting a home, not reading a brochure.
Clean layout, lots of real photography, minimal text per section.
Not corporate. Not clinical. Not dramatic.
